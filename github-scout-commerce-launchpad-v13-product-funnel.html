          What usually happens when you wait

          • Apps stay installed because nobody owns the decision.
          • Duplicate tools quietly stack up across email, reviews, support, upsells, and subscriptions.
          • Page weight and script conflicts become a performance problem after the budget problem.
          • Agencies and devs end up pitching broad rebuilds instead of focused savings work.

          Is this replacing a full technical audit?

          No. It is the pre-audit that tells you where a full audit, replacement sprint, or custom build is actually worth the time.

          Does the scanner know every app?

          No scanner does. V13 focuses on visible storefront signals, pasted source, and practical spend logic. Unknown apps can still be added manually.

          Why include open-source or repo-backed paths?

          Because some app jobs are stable enough to replace with a controlled build, while others should stay SaaS. The audit separates those cases.

          What should an agency do with this?

          Run the scan on a sales call, send the report, then scope the highest-payback cleanup or replacement sprint.
